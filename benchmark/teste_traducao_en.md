# Chapter 1: The Last Lantern

Mara Vale tightened the strap of her weathered satchel and studied the bridge ahead. Half of its planks were missing, and the river below moved fast enough to carry a horse away.

“Tell me you have another route,” she said.

Theo Ardent unfolded the map for the third time. “I have another route.”

Mara waited. Wind rattled the old sign beside them.

“It takes four days,” he admitted.

“Then we cross here.” She tested the first plank with her boot. It groaned but held. “The observatory closes at sunset, and I did not hike through two storms to find a locked door.”

Theo looked at the dark clouds gathering behind the hills. “Technically, the second storm found us.”

A sharp crack cut through the air. Mara jumped back as a board dropped into the river and vanished in the foam.

“Still feeling technical?” she asked.

He folded the map with great care. “Not especially.”

They tied a rope between their waists and started across. Near the middle, the bridge swayed hard to the east. Mara froze. The lantern hanging from the far post flickered once, although no one had tended that road in years.

Someone was waiting on the other side.

“Did you see that?” Theo whispered.

Mara drew the short blade from her belt. “Yes. Keep moving, and do not look down.”
